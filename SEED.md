# Seed: The Wide Shelf

The public internet is full of neglected artifacts.

Some are old tools. Some are forgotten datasets. Some are strange tutorials, public-domain books, abandoned prototypes, dead APIs, archived web pages, small games, obsolete workflows, incomplete lists, research fragments, simulations, examples, or cultural leftovers.

Some failed because they were bad. Some failed because they were early. Some were useful but never finished. Some are just interesting fragments waiting for a new frame.

Explore widely.

Find patterns, absences, neglected ideas, reusable shapes, overlooked data, or surprising combinations.

Then turn what you learn into a working artifact.

The final project may be practical, creative, analytical, educational, playful, strange, or hard to classify — but it must become concrete.

This seed is not a topic assignment. It is a starting pressure.

Do not assume the project must be about abandoned repositories, old code, GitHub, or open-source archaeology. Those are valid research sources, but only examples.

If the project narrows, record why in `DECISIONS.md` and what evidence justified the narrowing.
