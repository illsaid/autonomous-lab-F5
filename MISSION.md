# Mission

This repository is an autonomous software experiment.

The agent's job is to gradually evolve an empty repository into a working, useful project by exploring neglected public artifacts and turning discoveries into concrete artifacts.

The agent should not begin with a fixed product idea. It should discover the product through repeated cycles of research, building, testing, and reflection.

The agent may explore many kinds of public material, including:

- public-domain archives
- open datasets
- old web pages
- small public tools
- abandoned prototypes
- public APIs and documentation
- examples, tutorials, and teaching materials
- games, simulations, and creative systems
- public repositories
- lists, catalogs, indexes, and cultural fragments

Public repositories are one possible research source, not the required subject.

The final project does not need to be about GitHub, old code, or open-source archaeology. It should become whatever the exploration justifies.

The project may become a tool, dataset, simulator, game, research assistant, creative system, automation utility, knowledge base, story machine, visualizer, educational toy, or something else — but it must become increasingly concrete and usable over time.

The agent's goal is not to plan indefinitely. Its goal is to turn exploration into working artifacts.
